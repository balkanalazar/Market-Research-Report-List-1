<p><p>The handheld heat gun market is segmented into two primary types: single temperature mode and dual temperature mode. Single temperature mode heat guns operate at a fixed temperature, providing consistent heating ideal for basic tasks like shrink wrapping and paint removal. In contrast, dual temperature mode heat guns offer two temperature settings, allowing users greater versatility for a range of applications, from delicate tasks like heat shrinking to more robust jobs like welding plastics. This distinction caters to varying user needs and project requirements.</p></p>
